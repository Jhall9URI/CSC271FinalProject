The Basics

Before you begin playing poker, you need to understand the rules and the terminology. Refer to the following sections:

Part 1: Objectives/Rules

In Poker, your goal is to have the best 5-card hand as possible. Here, we are focusing on the most popular form of poker, Texas Hold'em. In Texas Hold'em, each player gets dealt two cards face-down, and there are also 5 "community cards" in the middle of the table that every player can use. After Several rounds of betting, all of the remaining players reveal their hand, and the best hand wins.

Hand Rankings:

  1. Royal Flush: (10 J Q K A) of the same suit. the best hand in poker.
  2. Straight Flush: 5 consecutive cards, all of the same suit.
  3. Four of a Kind: 4 cards of the same value.
  4. Full House: 3 cards with one value, and 2 cards of another value.
  5. Flush: 5 cards with the same suit
  6. Straight: 5 consecutive cards in order. Ace can be considered a high or low card.
  7. Three of a kind: 3 cards with the same value.
  8. Two Pair: two seperate pairs of cards.
  9. One Pair: two cards that are the same value.
  10. High Card: if none of the above, the highest card in your hand matters.

Part 2: Player Actions/Order of Events.

  • (PREFLOP): The players to the left of the dealer pay the blinds, then all players are dealt two hole cards. A round of betting occurs.
  • (FLOP): After the Preflop betting, 3 cards are dealt in the center of the table, all players can use these cards. another round of bettng occurs.
  • (TURN): A fourth card is place in the middle, and another round of betting occurs
  • (RIVER): A fifth and final card is dealt in the middle and a final round of betting occurs.
  • (SHOWDOWN): All remaining players reveal their hands, the best hand wins the entire pot.
