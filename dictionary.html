Definition
tests

A

Aggressive: a playstyle that revolves around betting and raising often, instead of checking/calling

All In: putting all of your chips into the pot. Once you are all in, you cannot make any more bets/calls for the remainder of the hand.

Ante: a forced bet that every player must make at the beginning of the hand.

B

Backdoor: a hand that is completed by using both the turn and the river community cards. Primarily used in Texas Holden or Omaha

Bad Beat: having the (sometimes overwhelmingly) favored hand, and still losing due to poor luck.

Bankroll: the amount of money that one has available to play poker with.

Bet: voluntarily putting money into the pot. Other players must call the bet or fold.

Blinds: two forced bets made by the two players to the left of the Dealer Button. The first blind—the small blind—is usually half the amount of the other one—the Big Blind.

Bluff: The act of a player betting out and trying to win the pot when they don’t believe that they have the best hand.

Button: also called the dealers button, it is a marker that gets passed around the table clockwise after every hand. The player that currently has the button gets to act last during every round of betting, a distinct advantage.

C

Call: Matching another player’s bet/raise amount.

Check: passing your turn without making any bet. This option is only available if there has been no bets earlier in the round of betting.

Community Cards: the cards in the middle of the board in Texas Hold’em and Omaha. These cards can be used by any player in the hand.

Continuation Bet: Betting a second time after betting/raising during the previous round.

E

Equity: your potential share of the pot, once you factor in your probability of winning the pot with your current hand.

F

Fish: a poor/unprofitable player.

Flop: the first 3 community cards. The Flop is revealed afte the first round of betting finishes.

Flush: A hand where all 5 cards are of the same suit.

Fold: give up the current hand and forfeit any chips already put into the pot.

Full House: also called a “full boat” or a “boat”. a hand consisting of a pair and a three of a kind.

H

High-Low: a variant of poker where half of the pot goes to the best hand, and half of the pot goes to the player with the lowest 5-card hand. For example, A2345 is the best low-hand you can get.

Hole Cards: The cards that each player receives at the start of a hand. Hole cards can only be used by the player they were dealt to.

I

Implied odds: Like Pot Odds, but also takes into account the possible chips added to pot later on in the hand.

L

Limit: a maximum bet or raise allowed for a hand or a round of betting.

Limit Hold'em: A format of Texas Hold'em where players may only bet a specific amount at any one time.

Limp: Calling the big blind during the opening round of betting.

Loose: a playstyle that consists of playing many hands. Not to be confused with “Aggressive”, which means to bet/raise often.

M

Muck: concede and hand without revealing your cards.

N

No-Limit Hold'em: a format of Texas Hold'em that places no restrictions on the size of bets.

Nuts: the best possible hand for a given situation.

O

Odds: The probability of winning/losing a hand.

Omaha: a form of poker where each player gets 4 hole cards as well as 5 community cards.

Open-Ended Straight Draw: a straight draw where a player can complete their straight either from the low end or the high end. (for example, a player with 2345 can complete a straight with an Ace or a 6).

Outs: the cards a player needs to win a hand. Usually used when referring to an all-in player that currently has the worse hand.

P

Passive: A Playstyle where one does not play many hands. The opposite of a "loose" playstyle.

Pocket Pair: a term in Texas Hold’em where a player has two hole cards of the same value.

Pot: the total amount of money that each player has committed to the hand. The winner(s) of the hand receive the pot.

Pot Odds: an advanced strategy that compares the amount of money in the pot to the player’s chances of winning the hand. If a player bets $10 and has a 50% chance to win a $30 pot, then they have good Pot odds because they will—on average—win more than they lose in that situation.

R

Rake: a small portion of a pot that the casino takes from each pot, as a fee.

Raise: Making an increase to another player’s previous bet.

River: the fifth and final community card.

Royal Flush: a hand consisting of 10, J , Q, K, A, all of the same suit. The best possible hand in Poker.

S

Street: the cards dealt during a particular round of betting. i.e. in holdem, the streets are the Flop, the Turn, and the River.

Straight: a 5-card hand that consists of 5 cards in contiguous increasing order.

T

Texas Hold'em: a form of poker where each player receives 2 hole cards and all players share 5 community cards.
